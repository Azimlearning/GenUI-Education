## 1. Overview

hackAstone is a global hackathon designed specifically around university capstone (CAPstone) projects. It bridges academic project work with real-world AI innovation for education. Any tertiary student enrolled in any higher learning institution worldwide is eligible to participate. Institutions with credit-bearing CAPstone projects are particularly encouraged to join.

The contest runs over multiple months, culminating in a final presentation and award ceremony at a physical conference in Amsterdam, Netherlands.

---

## 2. Theme

### "Agentic AI for Education"

Participating teams are required to **build AI Agents** targeting one or more of the following educational domains:

- Higher Education
- K-12 Education
- Preschool & Special Needs Education
- Vocational & Professional Education
- Lifelong Learning

Teams have full freedom to design any type of AI agent system within the education space — the scope is intentionally broad to encourage diverse and creative solutions.

## 4. Participation Rules

### Team Composition
- Maximum **8 qualified students** from **1 or 2 universities** per team.
- Students must be currently matriculated in a tertiary institution.
- Teams can be formed from **two universities across different cities, countries, or continents** (international collaboration is explicitly encouraged).
- **Interdisciplinary teaming** (e.g., engineering + education + computer science) is also encouraged.

### University Role
- Each university must have an **authorised university advisor** who manages nomination.
- Each university can recommend a **maximum of 3 teams** to advance to the semi-finals.
- The advisor is the official point of contact between the university and the organiser.

### Registration
- Registration is **free of charge**.
- All registered teams receive an **official certificate** from the organiser upon completing their hackAstone project, regardless of advancement to semi-finals.

## 6. Judging Criteria

Teams are evaluated across four equally important dimensions:

### 01 — Innovation & Novelty
The degree to which the submission demonstrates **original thought**, creative ingenuity, and a meaningful departure from existing solutions within its problem domain.
- Focus: **Originality of Concept**

### 02 — Technical Readiness Level
The maturity, robustness, and operational functionality of the implementation, assessed through engineering integrity, completeness of build, and demonstrable performance.
- Focus: **Build Quality & Execution**

### 03 — Commercial Viability
The potential of the solution to evolve into a **sustainable commercial venture or market-ready product**, evidenced by a coherent value proposition and credible pathway to adoption.
- Focus: **Market Potential**

### 04 — Educational Significance & Applicability
The submission's contribution to **learning outcomes** and its capacity for meaningful deployment within real-world educational contexts, advancing both pedagogical value and practical utility.
- Focus: **Pedagogical Impact**
